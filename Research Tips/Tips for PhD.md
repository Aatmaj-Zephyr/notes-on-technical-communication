### General gyan
1. Problems are always interdisiplinary
2. You will have to learn new things to solve the problem. Be ready to learn new things.  Never be afraid to learn new things. You are doing PhD to learn new things. If you are not learning new things, you are doing something wrong.
3. Go to basics
4. PhD is like Guru - Shishya
5. Never ask the guide "what should I do next" Always come prepared with some ideas and ask "what do you think about these ideas"
6. Learn to ask the right questions.
7. Dont put things under the carpet. If you dont know anything - go and ask AI/Google/prof/friend
8. Dont be afraid to ask "stupid" questions. There is no such thing as a stupid question. If you have a doubt, ask it. It is better to ask and clear your doubt than to keep it in your mind and let it grow into a bigger problem.
9. Ask for tips from your seniors. Ask them what mistakes they did and what they would do differently if they had to do it again. This will help you to avoid making the same mistakes and also to learn from their experience.
10. Failure is part of life
11. It is a good idea to frequenctly connect with your old colleagues, professors and friends. This will help you to stay connected with the outside world and also to get new ideas and perspectives. It will also help you to get feedback on your work and also to get support when you are going through a tough time. Show them. your work and get their feedback. They may have a different perspective on your work. A good way would be to do mock presentations with them and get their feedback. This will help you to improve your presentation skills as well as to get feedback on your work. It will also help you to get new ideas and perspectives on your work.

### Document everything
1. Unlike Masters or Bachelor's work, in PhD you are going to need what you are going to do now after four years. You don't know what things are important and what things are not important at this stage. Hence we have to document everything such that you will be able to just pick up from where you left off even after four years. This requires a lot of discipline as well as turning on that metal switch that you need to note down everything. 
2. Document everything in a way that you can understand it after four years. This is very important. You should be able to understand your own work after four years. This will help you to continue your work without any disruption. It will also help you to write your thesis and also to write research papers based on your work.
3. Document everything in a way that others can understand it. So that the new person who is going to take over your work after you are done with your PhD can understand it and continue from where you left off. Or some new intern/student who has joined under you can work with you without you needing to handhold him.
4. Document every decision that you have taken. For example, you might have decided to not explore a particular idea. In that case, you should note it down. Otherwise, again after 2 years, when you come back to this, you will again think, "Why I haven't explored this area" and start doing it again. This will lead to a lot of rework and waste of time. Hence, it is better to note down the decision that you have taken and the reason behind it. This will help you to avoid doing the same thing again and again and also to save a lot of time.
5. Document every failed attempt. This will help you to avoid doing the same thing again.
6. Presenting your failures to your guide/committee is a great way of impressing that you have done a lot of work. It shows that you have explored a lot of ideas and have taken a lot of efforts. 
7. Maintain your notes & scribbles (both physical and digital) dont throw them away.
8. If you are working on hardware, take good photos of overall view of the hardware. and not just the details. Do this for every step. These are not only for reference but also for presentation.
9. Whenever you go for any conference, meeting etc, take good photos of yourself which can be posted on social media. Preferably take these with geotag on.

### Ergnomic setup
1. Monitor + good keyboard (pref mechanical) + mouse (pref. one with additional side keys) is must
2. Dont underestimate power of mouse while making diagrams and PPTs
3. Your setup should be ergniomic and comfortable for you
4. Stand up and walk after sometime. Follow the 20-20-20 rule, take care of your health.
5. There should be plenty of light in your setup

3. PhD is a marathon and not a sprint
4. Time spent wasting is not wasted time.

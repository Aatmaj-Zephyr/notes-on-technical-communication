# General tips
1. Defending thesis = Marketting
2. Papers and ideas should give you a 'feeling' this 'feeling' should be conveyed to your audience
3. Even though you know your content like the back of your hand, your audience may be hearing your content for the first time. It is new for them.
4. Talk should not try to be comprehensive or cover all the points. Report/thesis/paper should be comprehensive. Talk should intrigue the people to read the report.
5. Talk should be like a story, with a beginning, middle and end. It should have a flow. It should be engaging. It should be interesting. It should be easy to follow.



### Practice is the key

1. Practice is the key.
2. Practice multiple times in front of friends (mock presentations). Rehearse many times. With friends. With advisor.
3. Practice with self using google slides presenter practice mode: Measure time taken
4. Practice using the same material (slides/whiteboard) as will be available in front of the final presentation
5. It is a good idea to speak with an audience member (a friend) just before giving the talk. Warms you up.


### Speaking guidelines

1) Dont worry if you are nervous.
2) Speak loud and clear  - even with a mic, using a `low, private conversational voice’  does not work for presentations.
3) Imagine you are speaking without a mic in a small room (15-20 people), use that volume.
4) Face the audience, make eye contact, should look at different people at different times. Do not look at the same person (e.g. your guide) the whole time
5) Do not look at the laptop screen while presenting, alternate between looking at and pointing to the projector display screen  and  looking at the audience.
6) Stand at a position and at an angle that you can look  at and/or point to the projected display and look  at the audience
7) If screen is too large and not easy to point to, ensure you have a laser pointer
8) If stage is too large and laptop too far away, make sure you have a slide advancing remote
9) Always start by asking the audience if you are audible.
10) Smile. When you smile, others feel you are the master of your subject.
11) Rookie speakers continue without taking pauses. Master speakers know the importance of pause. Rookies feel that if they pause, people feel that they have forgotton their speech. But actually pause is a great way of bringing emphasis on a particular thing.

### Time management

1. If someone is asking a lot of doubts in a particular section, it is better to move on to the next section and answer the doubts at the end of the presentation. 
2. Leave enough time for questions at the end. (if you want to take questions that is 😈) Tell that rest questions will be taken 'offline' ie after the presentation.
3. Add buffers to speed up or slow down as required. Practice them.



### Answering questions

1. Prepare FAQs and make slides (supplementary slides) for them
2. practice, colect FAQs
3. Collect questions that can be asked from LLMs
4. More you sweat during peace,  less you bleed during war - go collect more probable questions that can be asked and add them to slides
5.Clarification Questions: I didnt follow your definition, what if ...? Should be answered immediately.
6. Argumentative/Evaluative Questions: What is your opinion of xyz method? Better to defer to the end. Politely say so
